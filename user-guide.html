What Is This Tool?

The Suncare POG Lookup app is a planogram reference tool built to help field teams quickly identify, locate, and verify suncare products on seasonal pallet and endcap displays. It consolidates the planogram PDFs, product images, UPC data, and shelf positions that our teams already work with every day ‚Äî and puts it all in one fast, searchable, mobile-friendly location. No new data is collected or created; the app simply organizes existing resources so any team member can access it.

1 Getting Started ‚Äî Store Selection

When you open the app, the landing screen asks you to select your store. Each store is mapped to the correct planogram layout (4-sided pallet or endcap) so you always see the right configuration.

1 Tap the Select Store‚Ä¶ dropdown at the center of the screen.
2 Choose your store number from the list. A preview card will appear showing the planogram name, POG number, live date, and SKU count.
3 Tap Load Planogram ‚Üí to enter the main view. The app loads all products, shelf positions, and images for your assigned display.
Tip
If you need to switch stores later, tap X in the top-right corner of the header.

2 Browsing the Display ‚Äî Shelf View

The Browse tab is the default view. It renders a visual representation of every shelf on the current bay, with products scaled to approximate their real-world proportions.

Understanding the Layout

Shelves are displayed top-to-bottom, matching the physical display. Each product tile shows the item image and its position number. Status badges indicate special designations:

BadgeMeaning
NEW Newly added to the planogram ‚Äî not on the previous version
SRP Shelf-Ready Packaging ‚Äî product ships in its display tray

3 Navigating Bays (4-Sided Pallet)

The 4-sided pallet display has four bays. There are two ways to switch between them:

Option A ‚Äî Bay Buttons

Tap any Bay 1 through Bay 4 button in the navigation bar fixed to the bottom of the screen. The active bay is highlighted in gold.

Option B ‚Äî Two-Finger Swipe

Place two fingers on the shelf area and swipe left or right to move to the next or previous bay. A pop-up notification confirms which bay you've navigated to.

How It Works
The two-finger gesture is intentional ‚Äî single-finger scrolling is reserved for scrolling within a shelf row, so bay switching requires two fingers to prevent accidental navigation.

4 Scanning a Product Barcode

The fastest way to identify a product is to scan its UPC barcode using your device's camera.

1 Tap the Scan tab at the top of the screen. The camera will activate automatically.
2 Point the camera at the product's barcode. Align it within the scanning area on screen. The scanner supports barcodes in any orientation.
3 When recognized, the app immediately displays the product detail card showing the item name, image, UPC, bay number, shelf location, position, and facings.
Tip
If you're scanning in a dimly lit area, tap the flashlight button (bottom-right of the camera view) to toggle your device's torch for additional lighting.
Item Not Found?
If the scanned item is not on the current planogram, the app will display a notification. If the item was removed from the planogram, a red warning card will appear indicating as much.

5 UPC Search ‚Äî Type to Find

If scanning isn't practical, you can search by typing part of the UPC or the product name.

1 Tap the UPC tab to open the search screen.
2 Type at least 4 digits of the UPC (the last four digits work great) or begin typing the product name. Results appear automatically as you type.
3 A list of matching products appears with thumbnail images, names, and locations. Tap the correct product to open its detail card.
Example
Holding a Sun Bum Cool Down Lotion? Type 0047 (the last four digits of UPC 87176000047) and the app will show it in the results. Tap to see exactly where it goes.

Location Info

Bay number, shelf number, position on the shelf, and the number of facings ‚Äî everything needed to place the item correctly.

Mini Planogram

A miniature shelf map of the entire bay with the current product highlighted in gold, so you can see its position in context at a glance.

Quick Actions

View Planogram opens the official POG PDF. Scan UPC jumps back to the camera. See Location navigates to the item on the shelf view and highlights it.

7 Built-In PDF Planogram Viewer

Opening the PDF

1 Tap the POG PDF chip in the filter bar, or tap View Planogram from any product detail card.
2 The PDF opens in a full-screen viewer with a toolbar at the top.

9 Data Transparency

Where Does the Data Come From?

Every piece of information in this app comes directly from existing Retail Odyssey resources that are already distributed to field teams. The app does not generate, collect, or transmit any new data. It is a read-only reference tool ‚Äî an alternate way to access what we already have.

DataSource
Product names, UPCs, positions, facingsOfficial planogram data
Product imagesManufacturer-provided product photography
Planogram PDFsSame PDFs distributed via existing channels
Store-to-POG mappingStandard store assignment lists
NEW / SRP badgesDerived from planogram

The app works entirely offline after the initial load ‚Äî no login is required, no personal data is collected, and no information is sent to any external server. It is simply an organized presentation of the same planogram data every team member already has access to.
